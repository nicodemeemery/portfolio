Hydrofoil Study

With my friend Amédée, we studied the advantages of hydrofoil on a boat. We had to study the principle of similarities to be able to build a 3D model adapted to the boat we were studying.

We also set up a water flow system using an electric motor. We found it very difficult to control the flow, especially in terms of speed and current line management. Indeed, it's important to keep the flow as laminar as possible, which isn't easy in this case.

Once this had been achieved, an experiment using a dynamometer enabled us to determine the boat's lift and drag.

One of the main difficulties was to quantify the efficiency of the foil's lift, because when speed increases there are 2 phenomena. The foil's lift increases, so the boat rises, so buoyancy decreases. So there's a question mark hanging over the equation, since we have an equation with 2 unknowns.

Nevertheless, the results have been conclusive, and we're now going to look at the precise distribution of vertical actions between the boat's buoyancy and the foil's lift.

Tire pressure overriding control project

My friend Corentin and I have been working on a pressure control system for bicycle tires.

Our project is part of our shared passion for the world of cycling and competition. In the run-up to the legendary Paris-Roubaix race, cyclists are looking for the best pressure to maximize their performance between the various cobbled sections. The challenge is to find a compromise between the right pressure for the road and the right pressure for the cobbles.

With our project, we want to make it possible to instantly control the pressure to achieve the ideal pressure zone.
